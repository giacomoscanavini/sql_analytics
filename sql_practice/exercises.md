# Expanded SQL Exercises

## shop.db

1. Show all customers, including those who have never placed an order.
2. Find products that have never been ordered.
3. Calculate revenue per order, excluding cancelled, refunded, and pending orders.
4. Find customers with zero completed-order revenue.
5. Find the top 5 products by completed-order revenue.
6. Find categories with at least 2 products but less than 100 completed revenue.
7. Compare gross revenue versus completed revenue by month.
8. Find orders that used promo codes.
9. Find customers whose city is unknown.
10. Rank customers by completed revenue, handling ties correctly.
11. Find discontinued products that were still ordered.
12. Calculate average order value by country.
13. Find products with zero revenue despite appearing in an order.
14. Find customers who placed only cancelled/refunded/pending orders.
15. Create a view named `completed_order_revenue`.

## hr.db

1. Show departments with no employees.
2. Show employees not assigned to any project.
3. Show inactive employees and their project assignments.
4. Calculate average salary by department, excluding inactive employees.
5. Find employees earning exactly the same salary as someone else.
6. Rank employees by salary within department.
7. Find managers with at least 2 direct reports.
8. Find projects with zero budget.
9. Find projects with no assigned employees.
10. Calculate total allocated hours by project.
11. Find departments whose active payroll exceeds 150000.
12. Find employees earning above their department average.
13. Calculate each employee's salary percentage of department payroll.
14. Find locations with more than one department.
15. Compare employee count and active employee count by department.

## streaming.db

1. Show users with no plays.
2. Show tracks that were never played.
3. Show artists with no played tracks.
4. Find plays where zero seconds were played.
5. Count plays by device, including unknown devices.
6. Calculate total listening time by user, including users with zero listening time.
7. Find explicit tracks and their total plays.
8. Rank tracks by total seconds played.
9. Find each user's favorite genre by listening time.
10. Find users whose country is unknown.
11. Calculate completion rate for each play: seconds_played / duration_seconds.
12. Find tracks whose average completion rate is below 50%.
13. Compare active versus inactive users by listening time.
14. Find genres with at least 3 tracks.
15. Calculate each artist's share of total listening time.
